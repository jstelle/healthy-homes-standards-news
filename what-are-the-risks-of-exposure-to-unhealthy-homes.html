What Are the Risks of Exposure to Unhealthy Homes?

Urban dwellers are increasingly aware of the risks associated with living in an unhealthy home. The indoor environment has a significant impact on our health and well-being, and can negatively impact our quality of life if it is not maintained at a healthy standard. An unhealthy home can increase the risk of contracting diseases such as allergies, asthma, or infections from poor indoor air quality (IAQ), or can lead to non-occupational injuries from falls or accidents due to inadequate lighting or other hazards. In addition, unhealthy homes can lead to lower property values and create a poor first impression on potential buyers. The good news is that there are many simple ways to improve the indoor air quality of your home and reduce these risks. By following these tips and making simple changes, you can help ensure your home is a safe and healthy environment for everyone who lives there.

What are the risks of exposure to unhealthy homes?

There are many risks associated with exposure to unhealthy homes. Dust and Dust Mites

Dust mites are microscopic organisms that thrive on human skin cells, pet dander, and organic materials such as plant matter, food, and fabrics. They are a common cause of allergies, particularly in young children, the elderly, and those with allergies. Dust mites thrive in warm, humid environments, and are found most commonly in living and sleeping areas. They come in two varieties: the domestic mite (Dermatophagia pteronnina) and the Asian mite (Dermatophagia farina). The best way to prevent dust mite infestation is by keeping the floors and rugs in your home clean and dry, and using a mite-proof mattress and pillow cover. Other effective measures include keeping the humidity low by using air conditioning in hot weather and installing a dehumidifier in cold weather.

Molds and Mildew

Molds and mildew are a natural part of the environment and can be found in damp, dark spaces, such as basements, attics, and crawl spaces. They thrive in warm, moist environments, and can grow anywhere there is organic material such as water, food, and fabrics. The best way to prevent mold and mildew growth is to keep the floors and walls in your home dry, clean, and out of the sun. You can also clean mold and mildew by using a bleach and water solution. If you have a mold problem, you can use a dehumidifier or air purifier to remove the moisture and reduce the humidity in your home.
